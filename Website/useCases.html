Centralized Food Ordering Portal: Virginia Tech

Use Cases

The primary intended user of this Food Ordering Portal is VT Dining services. Primarily all the VT dining halls under dining services including Southgate Dining Hall. The secondary user will include the different Vendors who are interested in selling their produce to Southgate. This system will try to automate the processes that Southgate manager is doing manually thereby saving 2-3 hrs of Southgate Manager on a day to day basis.

  1. Southgate manager acts as the backbone of the VT dining Services. The main tasks of Southgate Manager includes, taking orders from all the dining halls for day to day basis. After retrieving orders from the dining halls, the combined order is placed to a vendor via Foodpro web service. (FoodPro is a paid subscription where you have to email the vendor for placing the order) If there are any unforeseen circumstances, the manager has to manually mail every dining hall to inform them about the intricacies. At 1 PM everyday Manager has to manually create the labels for the product for the next day using MS WORD. This whole manual process usually takes about 2-3 hrs daily. If there was an automated system to address this, we could easily save 2-3 hrs daily.

  2. Dining Hall managers have to manually take the orders from each and every food counter manager to get the consolidated amount required for each of the dining halls. Then, he has to mail his requirements to the Southgate manager before 12:30 PM everyday. If there are any special requirements, the special requirements have to be mailed to the Southgate Manager 2 days before the actual need. If he needs to cancel any order, he has to call the Southgate Office and let them know the cancellations over the phone call. The usual day of managers includes at least 2 hrs of manual work which if automated can do wonders for them.

    3. (Figure 3) Use Case Diagram - Dining Halls
  3. Vendors who are interested in selling their produce to Southgate Hall, have to be dependent on the third party food app service FoodPro. Everyday, they will get an email from the Southagte Manager with the list of required produce for the next day. If the vendor has the product in the warehouse, he has to reply to the Southgate Manager to accept the order. The cut off time to send the items to Southgate Dining hall is 6 AM in the morning. If they do not have all the items requested, they cannot accept the order. The system works like a 0/1 knapsack. If the vendor has the partial requested order, he cannot accept the order.
